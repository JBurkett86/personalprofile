Project Idea

Overview

FairPaid - How much do you think you should be paid? – A website that allows you to compare pay rates and entitlements for similar job roles in your location.

This is a common question everyone has on their mind when taking on a new job or role and is also asked after so many years if your pay rate increases aren’t keeping up with inflation.
Some industries in Australia don’t have an award or enterprise agreement that gives a standardised pay rate and levels of pay for years’ experience (scaling awards). Employees can also be coerced into not discussing wages with their fellow employees which can lead to pay inequality even within the same organisation.

Motivation

When applying for a new job, for the same job at a competitor what is the expected Pay Rate? Over time rates? Penalty rates? Holiday or Leave entitlements? Parental leave? Are you being paid less than others with the same role at this organisation?

This website allows you to answer all these questions and more from a user contributed database that is verified and pooled based on all others that have provided similar information. Then others can search based on their job description, location and employer size too see what everyone else paid in a similar role as well as other entitlements.

An example of an existing product that does something similar would be PayScale.com a website that uses industry data to determine best priced contacts for employers, but our website is designed and focused on the employee. Other sites like Indeed.com.au and Seek.com.au can give you estimated pay scales for jobs in certain locations, but the pay scales they show have large variations and don’t include other entitlements.
The Australian government also does this, poorly. If you are paid against an award, congratulations you already have access to this information and you probably have a strong union behind it keeping this up to date. However, if a private agreement is used or you’re not part of a union, you could just be using the National Employment Standards, which are minimum at best and don’t explore pay rates above minimum wage.
If you want to see what the current going pay rate is for you role and location or you work in an industry that doesn’t have standard industry awards or publicly available enterprise agreements then this is the site for you.

The main different between this site and competitors is that is user contributed, allowing for a much larger reach, resulting in bigger data sets that can give more accurate information. Of course, the user contributed data must be verified - with using state of the art image processing and machine learning techniques, taking a photo of your pay stub from a phone can be uploaded and verified against existing or similar entries.

There is also a very global need for a site like this. This will give employees the ability to easily unionise or individually negotiate pays as all this information will be available to them. There are many countries that aren’t as lucky as Australia with our employment standards, and this website could close the income inequality that is increasing daily across the globe.

Description

FairPaid will be a website that will allow users to login for free, input their current job details, pay info and a snapshot of their pay slip to be added to the database, then allow them to search for similar jobs and see what the going pay rates are for them in their location (state or country) based on what others have contributed. Names and personal information won’t be included in the search results for privacy, purely averages for pay rates or listed rates as well as details for any entitlements (holiday days per year, etc).
The login will not remain free after a month, they can choose to pay a small monthly fee to continue access searches or pay to access searches of different jobs/ industries.

A minimum viable product will consist of a base webapp with:
Secure User login, Detailed storage of someone’s Job Description, Industry, Employer information, Location (e.g. city) and a list of entitlements options (Holidays, Sick Days, Parental Leave Days, Allowances, etc). Photo storage of the employees paid stubs (allowing to blur out any personal details they don’t want to disclose such as bank details or TFNs) will be the key. Validation of this information will need to be done manually in the beginning. Having human verified data sets will be of higher quality and allow for better machine learning results for later versions.
Version 2 will have improved machine learning to better process images of pay slips to remove or significantly reduce the manual data verification.
It will also be used to find which large key jobs or industries are lacking user contribution so potential high traffic jobs can be targeted for promotion to fill these gaps.

It will also require some data sets to exist for it to go ‘live’. If no one has contributed, then there will be no perceived benefit of the site.
Initial user generated content is key for this products success. This can be achieved by grassroots campaigning of the site initially with a small market pull on places like Reddit and collaborating with unions to help pre-fill some of the information. Targeting a key area and business type or specific large employer would be ideal especially if as population sizes for a large employer without unions would allow for larger data set.
Then promotion through a small social media campaign targeted at this specific location (Facebook/ Reddit), then followed up by a traditional media campaign of news stories for any success of a new or existing employee at the targeted business as part of an official launch. These success stories promoting the site will bring in more user contribution, resulting in more success stories and more user contribution and on and on.
Promoting globally from launch is mandatory, if there is going to be success it must hit as many areas as possible.

How is FairPaid going to make money or sustain itself? FairPaid will follow a subscription-based model on a low dollar per month basis (i.e $5 a month) allows them to be able to search in a key industry for free for the month, additional industry searches are at an increased cost. User contributions will allow for a free months’ worth to promote users updating or adding new information for their industry. Then allow for them to cancel when they don’t need it (they got a job or no longer looking).
Other opportunities are available for advertising marketing from within the app. E.g. Cut a deal with leading Superannuation funds in specific industries, then promote those super funds when the user is contributing for that industry. Other promotion options could be available with banks, or payment services like Afterpay.
There will also be opportunities to collaborate with unions, allowing them a higher paid subscription to give broader searches to give them ammo when negotiating with employers.

Tools and Technologies

The team will use Agile methodology, breaking the software development cycle into smaller chunks This enable the development team to compartmentalise and work to their strengths with small interactive tasks, while getting continuous feedback leading to a better working environment.

Using a Domain Driven Design to link the product design to the core business concept will ensure the product can be transitioned to any other technology easily without any major disruption. If a change in database engine, retirement of old technology occurs, the core design of the product using DDD lets it be easily transferred. It also benefits should a shift in technology be required due to expansion of the product that needs are not met with current technology.
It will also allow for quick and easy, sale or transfer of the product to another entity or rebrand and re-launch should it be needed. The code and design of the product will be easy to pick up and build upon should another team take over. Location of branding, product name, etc from within the product can be easily changed with this design and allow a fast turnaround should a re-launch be required.

The web app will be managed, designed, and documented using Microsoft DevOps. Using Task Boards in DevOPs configured to the Agile methodology, sprints will be created and user stories outlining each piece of requirement. This will keep clear and concise requirements and allow scalability for team size.

The communication between the team will be through Microsoft Teams, allowing for remote working environment in the current climate. It gives the team remote file storage, ability to run presentations internally and with stakeholders. And is future proof with the organisational growth connecting people between cities, states and even countries.

The website will be built as a webapp using .NetCore 5 being written in C#. This is the most up to date cutting edge development option that is easy to develop with for any team members with different levels of experience.
It will be published to the web via Microsoft Azure. A Continuous Integration deployment plan will be designed and implemented using Microsoft DevOps to fulfill this.
This will allow for faster deployment of updates, assurance of quality of release with automated unite testing resulting in an overall streamlined development process.

User login will use Microsoft Identity, allows for the user to authenticate with their existing Microsoft, Facebook or Google accounts.
It will meet high security requirements with Multi Factor Authentication due to the sensitivity of data that is stored.

Data storage will initially use Azure DevOps SQL data as it is cost effective and will meet country security requirements. Data storage will be split across different countries to meet local regulations and allow for better performance of data access when it is localised.
The design of the product using DDD will allow for the data storage options to be changed should feasibility, cost or availability become an issue.
